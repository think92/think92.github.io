About

I am a Postdoc at Johns Hopkins University since January, 2020 (Research Adviser: Dr. Margaret E. Johnson). My current research focuses on computational reaction-diffusion dynamics. I received my PhD in physics from Institute of Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences at December, 2019. My research was the theoretical and computational study about the mechanism of Kinesin's movement at CAS Key Lab of Soft Matter and Biological Physics(SM1, Group Leader: Dr. Peng-Ye Wang). My thesis adviser is Dr. Ping Xie.
